All Obesity Surgery Procedures Available

Prof. Dr. Gerhard Prager offers a complete range of modern bariatric surgery techniques, each designed to achieve significant and sustainable weight loss. The optimal procedure for you depends on your current weight, health status, dietary preferences, and long-term goals.

Gastric Bypass (RYGB)

The gold standard in bariatric surgery. Creates a small pouch and bypasses part of the intestine, achieving 70-80% excess weight loss. Proven long-term outcomes.

Sleeve Gastrectomy

Reduces stomach volume by 80%. Minimally invasive, preserves anatomy, and achieves 60-70% excess weight loss. Popular first-line procedure.

Omega-Loop Bypass

Advanced variation of gastric bypass with simplified anatomy. Excellent for revision surgery and achieving consistent weight loss with fewer complications.

SADI-S (Single Anastomosis Duodenoileal Switch)

Simplified intestinal bypass procedure. Combines restrictive and malabsorptive effects. Ideal for patients seeking maximum weight loss with metabolic improvement.

BPD/DS (Biliopancreatic Diversion with Duodenal Switch)

Most powerful bariatric procedure for extreme obesity and metabolic disease. Combines restrictive and significant malabsorptive components for maximal weight loss.

Revision & Conversion Surgery

If you had previous weight loss surgery with suboptimal results, Prof. Prager specializes in safely converting or revising procedures to improve outcomes.

Frequently Asked Questions About Obesity Surgery in Europe

What is the difference between obesity surgery and weight loss surgery?
These terms are used interchangeably. "Bariatric surgery," "weight loss surgery," and "obesity surgery" all refer to surgical procedures designed to help people with severe obesity lose weight. These procedures work by reducing stomach size, bypassing part of the intestines, or both. For more detailed information on the science behind these procedures, see our comprehensive guide on the Medical University of Vienna site.
Am I a candidate for obesity surgery?
Generally, bariatric surgery is recommended for people with a BMI of 40 or higher, or BMI of 35+ with weight-related health conditions (diabetes, hypertension, sleep apnea). However, every case is individual. Prof. Prager will assess your specific situation during consultation. Even if you've had previous weight loss surgery, you may be a candidate for revision procedures.
How much weight can I expect to lose?
Weight loss varies by procedure and individual factors. Gastric Bypass typically results in 70-80% excess weight loss, Sleeve Gastrectomy 60-70%, and SADI-S/BPD-DS can achieve 80%+ excess weight loss. Prof. Prager's extensive research has documented these outcomes in detail.
How long does the surgery take?
Most procedures take 60-120 minutes, depending on complexity and anatomy. Prof. Prager uses minimally invasive (laparoscopic) techniques, which reduces operative time and recovery compared to open surgery. Hospital stay is typically 1-2 days for uncomplicated procedures.
What are the risks and complications?
All surgery carries some risk. Major complication rates (requiring reoperation) are 1-2% in experienced centers. Minor issues like constipation, nausea, or vitamin deficiencies are more common but manageable. Prof. Prager's extensive experience and careful patient selection minimize complications. Detailed risk assessment occurs during consultation.
Will I need to take vitamins for life after bariatric surgery?
Yes, lifelong vitamin and mineral supplementation is recommended, especially with bypass procedures. Your nutritionist will design a supplementation protocol tailored to your procedure and needs. Regular lab monitoring ensures you maintain optimal nutritional status. Our dedicated nutritional support ensures you have all the guidance needed. Read more on our nutrition guidance.
Can I get pregnant after obesity surgery?
Yes. In fact, obesity surgery can improve fertility and pregnancy outcomes. However, pregnancy timing and special nutritional management are important. It's generally recommended to delay pregnancy for 12-24 months after surgery to allow weight loss to stabilize. Prof. Prager will discuss family planning in detail during consultation.
What about loose skin after significant weight loss?
Significant weight loss can result in loose skin. This depends on age, skin elasticity, and amount of weight lost. Prof. Prager can discuss options including skin tightening procedures, which can often be arranged in Vienna. Some patients prefer to address this after their weight stabilizes, typically 1-2 years post-surgery.
How is obesity surgery different from diet and exercise alone?
For people with severe obesity, diet and exercise alone are rarely sufficient for significant, sustained weight loss. Bariatric surgery changes your brain's appetite regulation, limits food intake, and alters nutrient absorption — creating sustainable weight loss. The average person regains weight with diet alone, but post-bariatric surgery patients typically maintain 50%+ of weight loss long-term. Learn more in our detailed comparison.
