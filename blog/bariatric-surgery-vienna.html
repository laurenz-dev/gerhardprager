Bariatric Surgery Options in Vienna

Prof. Dr. Prager offers a comprehensive range of bariatric procedures, customized to each patient's needs:

Gastric Bypass (Magenbypass)

The most commonly performed bariatric procedure worldwide, combining restriction and malabsorption.

  • Creates a small pouch from the stomach
  • Bypasses part of the small intestine
  • Expected weight loss: 60-80% of excess weight
  • Procedure time: 90-120 minutes
  • Ideal for: High BMI, type 2 diabetes, severe reflux conditions

Sleeve Gastrectomy (Schlauchmagen)

A restrictive procedure that removes about 75-80% of the stomach.

  • Reduces stomach volume significantly
  • Preserves normal anatomy (reversible)
  • Expected weight loss: 50-70% of excess weight
  • Procedure time: 45-90 minutes
  • Ideal for: Lower-risk candidates, those preferring reversibility

Duodenal Switch

A more complex procedure combining restrictive and malabsorptive components.

  • Maximum weight loss among procedures
  • Expected weight loss: 70-85% of excess weight
  • Requires careful post-operative monitoring
  • Ideal for: Super-obese patients, revision cases

Revision and Revisional Surgery

For patients who have had previous bariatric surgery without satisfactory results.

  • Revision gastric bypass
  • Conversion to more restrictive procedures
  • Requires specialized expertise

The Bariatric Surgery Journey in Vienna

Phase 1: Pre-Operative Evaluation (2-4 weeks)

Comprehensive assessment to ensure candidacy and safety:

  • Initial consultation with Prof. Dr. Prager
  • Detailed medical history and BMI assessment
  • Blood work and laboratory tests
  • Cardiac evaluation (EKG, sometimes echocardiogram)
  • Upper GI imaging or gastroscopy
  • Psychological evaluation
  • Nutritional assessment

Phase 2: Pre-Operative Preparation (1-2 weeks)

Getting ready physically and mentally for surgery:

  • Pre-operative diet (usually 2 weeks before surgery)
  • Medication adjustments
  • Final consultations and questions
  • Admission planning and logistics

Phase 3: Hospital Stay (1-2 nights)

Modern minimally invasive surgery with quick recovery:

  • Admission on surgery day
  • Minimally invasive laparoscopic procedure
  • Post-operative monitoring
  • Same-day discharge or 1-night stay

Phase 4: Early Recovery (Weeks 1-4)

The first month is critical for proper healing:

  • Liquid diet progression (1-2 weeks)
  • Gradual return to soft foods
  • Pain management
  • Regular follow-up appointments
  • Starting light activity

Phase 5: Progressive Recovery (Months 2-3)

Transition to normal activities:

  • Gradual increase in food variety and portions
  • Return to normal dietary routine
  • Increase in physical activity
  • Regular follow-up monitoring

Phase 6: Long-Term Follow-Up (Years 1+)

Lifetime monitoring and support:

  • Regular appointments at 6, 12, and 24 months
  • Ongoing laboratory monitoring
  • Vitamin and mineral supplementation
  • Lifestyle and behavioral support
  • Management of any weight regain

What to Expect: Results and Outcomes

Weight Loss Timeline

  • First 3 months: 15-25% weight loss (often more)
  • First 6 months: 40-50% of excess weight

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is bariatric surgery safe?

Yes, modern bariatric surgery is very safe, especially when performed by experienced surgeons like Prof. Dr. Prager. Complication rates are low (1-2%), and mortality is extremely rare (<0.1%). The benefits typically far outweigh the risks for suitable candidates.

How much weight will I lose?

Weight loss varies by procedure and individual factors. Most patients lose 50-80% of their excess weight. Outcomes depend on surgery type, compliance with dietary guidelines, and lifestyle changes.

Can bariatric surgery be reversed?

Sleeve gastrectomy is technically reversible but difficult. Gastric bypass can theoretically be reversed but is complex. Most surgeons recommend considering these as permanent procedures.

What are the risks and complications?

Major risks are rare but include bleeding, infection, and leaks. Minor complications like nausea and dumping syndrome are more common. With Prof. Dr. Prager's experience, complication rates are well below average.

Can I get pregnant after bariatric surgery?

Yes, fertility often improves after bariatric surgery. However, pregnancy planning should be coordinated with your surgeon, and special attention is needed for nutrient absorption during pregnancy.

Do I need vitamins forever?

Yes, lifelong vitamin and mineral supplementation is important, especially with gastric bypass. Sleeve gastrectomy requires less supplementation but still some monitoring.

What happens if I don't follow the diet?

Significant weight regain is possible if dietary guidelines are not followed. About 10-20% of patients experience some regain. Success requires commitment to lifestyle changes.

Can I have the surgery if I have other medical conditions?

Most conditions can be managed, but some require special consideration. Comprehensive pre-operative evaluation ensures safety. Discuss all medical conditions during your consultation.
