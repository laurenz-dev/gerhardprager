Benefits of Laparoscopic Surgery

  • Smaller Incisions: Typically 2-4 small cuts instead of one large incision, reducing tissue trauma.
  • Faster Recovery: Most patients return to normal activities within 2-3 weeks versus 4-6 weeks for open surgery.
  • Less Pain: Minimal incision size means reduced post-operative pain and lower analgesic requirements.
  • Reduced Infection Risk: Smaller incisions mean lower risk of surgical site infections and complications.
  • Better Cosmetic Results: Tiny scars are virtually invisible, allowing for faster confidence restoration.
  • Shorter Hospital Stay: Most laparoscopic bariatric patients go home same day or next day.
  • Reduced Blood Loss: Minimally invasive approach preserves patient blood volume and reduces transfusion risk.
  • Lower Complication Rates: Advanced laparoscopic technique minimizes surgical complications and re-operation risk.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between laparoscopic and open bariatric surgery? ▼

Laparoscopic surgery uses small incisions (typically 2-4 cuts of 5-12mm) with a camera to visualize the operative field. Open surgery requires one larger incision (15-20cm). Laparoscopic surgery results in less pain, faster recovery, smaller scars, and reduced infection risk while achieving the same results.

How long is recovery after laparoscopic bariatric surgery? ▼

Most patients return to light activities within 1-2 weeks and normal activities within 3-4 weeks. Full recovery, including complete scar healing and return to full exercise, typically takes 6-8 weeks. This is significantly faster than open surgery recovery of 12+ weeks.

Is laparoscopic bariatric surgery safe? ▼

Yes, laparoscopic bariatric surgery is safe when performed by experienced surgeons like Prof. Prager. With nearly 10,000 procedures, Prof. Prager has extensive data on safety outcomes. Complication rates are generally lower than open surgery, with reduced infection risk, less bleeding, and shorter hospital stays.

What are the benefits of endocrine surgery by laparoscopy? ▼

Laparoscopic endocrine surgery (thyroid, adrenal, parathyroid) offers superior cosmetic results with minimal neck or abdominal scarring. The magnified view provided by the laparoscope improves precision and reduces nerve injury risk. Hospital stays are shortened and patients recover faster with less pain.

Can revisional bariatric surgery be done laparoscopically? ▼

Yes, Prof. Prager specializes in laparoscopic revisional bariatric surgery, correcting previous weight loss procedures that didn't achieve desired results. The laparoscopic approach minimizes additional trauma and achieves optimal outcomes with the same recovery benefits as primary laparoscopic surgery.
